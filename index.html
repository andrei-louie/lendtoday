What About Interest Rates?

We believe in being up front about costs of services offered by our direct lenders. That’s why we’re not afraid to let you know that short term loans can be and often are expensive.

As an illustrative example, if you borrowed say $500 for 14 days, and the APR was 450%, the amount of interest you would owe would be $86.30 USD.

APRs range and are based on the terms set forth by the payday loan direct lender, the allowable interest rates set forth by state regulations where you reside, and other factors such as your credit score, employment status, current income, etc.

All of our direct lenders will provide you with loan terms prior to you signing any commitment to take out financing. It is important that you read these terms in their entirety as they will make you aware of any and all interest and costs associated with the cash advance.

How Does Repayment Work?

Typically the amount you borrow is due in full with any interest and fees associated on the due date, which can range from days to months depending on the type of short term loan. For cash advance loans, most lenders will automatically charge your bank account for the amount owed in full on the due date, so it is important to plan ahead and make sure that you have sufficient funds in place to cover the payback on the due date to avoid any additional “overdraft” fees your bank might impose.

What if I can’t Pay Back on Time?

Look, we understand everyone gets a little behind from time to time. Its nothing to be ashamed of. However, if you know in advance that you will not be able to pay off your balance in full on the due date, it is extremely important to reach out to your lender ASAP to let them know. In some cases (depending on the lender and state regulations), they may be able to extend your loan or “roll it over” to another period. If you are able to get an extension, be sure to ask if there are any additional fees or finance charges associated with the extension.
